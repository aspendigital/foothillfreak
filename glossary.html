Extreme-ly Low Elevation Skiing Glossary
Confused about this elusive new sport? The Freaktionary is here to help.

Corn Snow: noun. Springtime snow condition in which the snowpack surface identically resembles the yellow kernels of corn on the cob. Extra good corn conditions are likely to resemble the "salt and pepper" corn variety, though some prefer "indian corn cob" snow conditions. Don't try eating it though because it tastes like brussel sprouts.

De-veining: verb. To loose control while riding a narrow patch of snow and spin off into the rocks or grass. See veining.

Freakin: verb. To hike, climb, ski, or generally flail about with skis or snowboards on low elevation hills with the main intent of finding snow and/or angled surfaces upon which to slide back down.

Hoar Frost: noun. The perfect eyeshadow for an 80's themed get together.

Punjabi sticks: noun. (From the Philipines). A pointed stick projecting out of the snowpack and up the hill. Found in natural environments and on angry property owners turf. Best avoided.

Rockin: verb. To ride your board or boards across snowless areas regardless of potential damage to equipment, environment, participant, or innocent bystanders. Example: Coming down that ridge she was really rockin.

Ski Boardz: noun. A snowboard that has been sawed through longitudinally and mounted with any type of ski bindings, thus creating a pair of low cost, low weight fat skis. The "z" on the end of "board" is an onomatopoeia indicating the fact that the board has been sawed, or "zzzzzzz"ed.

Skookum-Chuck: (From Pacific North West Native American) verb. To ram a large, outstanding pillow of snow with the sole intention of being catapulted into the stratosphere. Noun. A large pillow of snow that forms over humped objects either natural or man-made.

Thumb Tack: verb. When a snowboarder lands head first into a deep, soft snowpack and becomes lodged in place. Whatever you do, don't get thumb tacked!

Tush Pusher: noun. A device that you attach to your ass in order to ascend a mountain when you feel lazy or want to socialize. Also known as "chair lift".

Under Control: adv. A term thats meaning is continually debated and completely relative. One man's "under control" is another man's 50mph complete yardsale accompanied by expletives and/or blood curdling screams.

Veining: verb. To ski a narrow line of snow that is closely boardered by dry ground. In the foothills, long paths of snow are created by drifted snow that stay around long after the rest of the thin snow pack has melted.

Weiner Wacker: noun. A shrub that has been partially buried in snow so that it's wispy end is crotch level or higher. Found throughout the Wasatch in the early season and at lower elevations all season long. Depending on your disposition, either avoid them completely, or ski over them with one ski on each side. See also Punjab stick.
